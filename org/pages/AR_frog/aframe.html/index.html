How it works

There are a few steps that need to be prepared to fully enjoy the "Pierre experience".

Hiro Marker

For augmented reality to work, we need a marker to place Pierre on. Pierre will react and follow this marker. Here is where Hiro comes in. Hiro is a marker used by the A-frame software tracking the contours and visualizing our beloved little critter.

It is easiest to print Hiro out, but it is also possible to open it on another screen while a second device is used to run the augmented reality visualisation. Sounds a bit "device-ception" but it does the trick!

Click on Hiro

Click and print the picture.

Open Pierre

Open the Pierre tab and point your camera to Hiro to see the magic happen.

A quick tour

When Hiro is printed out and the camera is pointed at it, you should be able to see Pierre. However, just seeing our froggy little friend is not enough! If you click on the upper left corner a dropdown will appear with three choices. Default, which will bring all of Pierres colorsettings back to it's default settings. BodyParts (figure A), which will give an overview of all of Pierre's body parts. If a body part is chosen by clicking on it, the third option will open up. Coloring (figure B), where a color and opacity can be chosen for the selected body part. This is done via an HSL coloring method. The upper bar is Hue, middle is Saturation and the lowest bar is Lightness. Just be sure to press Apply after the choice is made!
